BUILDER AUTHORIZATION
Montage Audio Qualification Repair
Status: APPROVED

This authorization revises the architectural unit of audio qualification. It is
not a threshold adjustment.

1. DESIGN DECISION

A cinematic moment remains the public composition object. It is not
simultaneously the smallest placement unit, the audio qualification unit, and
the failure boundary. Dialogue placement eligibility is local. Whole-moment
audio characteristics may influence scoring but must not invalidate unrelated
authored dialogue opportunities.

2. AUDIO QUALIFICATION HIERARCHY

Placement Safety evaluates only the dialogue interval, visual and audio
boundaries, required guard bands, transition overlap, and immediate local
context. Silence elsewhere in the parent moment is irrelevant to placement
safety.

Segment Assembly Safety evaluates the selected submoment or complete-shot
sequence for broken transitions, ghost speech, and unintended discontinuity.

Final Output Acceptance applies the strict dead-air rule to the encoded
montage. Intentional silence, room tone, ambience, fades, and dramatic pauses
must remain distinguishable from unintended output gaps.

3. SUBMOMENT CONSTRUCTION

When a parent moment fails preferred whole-moment audio qualification:

1. evaluate dialogue-bearing intervals independently;
2. expand only to safe complete-shot boundaries;
3. apply visual and audio guard bands;
4. preserve every independently safe placement;
5. reject only locally unsafe submoments.

4. DETERMINISTIC RESCUE

If ordinary qualification yields zero authored placements:

1. attempt normal cinematic-moment qualification;
2. evaluate dialogue-bearing intervals locally;
3. construct complete-shot placement submoments;
4. reject only locally unsafe placements;
5. relax preferred duration or placement density when necessary;
6. fail only when no complete-shot, locally audio-safe placement exists.

5. LONG-TAKE POLICY

When no safe internal boundary exists, use the complete take, choose another
eligible moment, relax target duration, or fail. Arbitrary duration cuts remain
forbidden.

6. WHOLE-MOMENT AUDIO METRICS

Whole-moment measurements remain scoring, ranking, and planning-preference
signals. They are not binary placement eligibility.

7. REQUIRED REPORTING

Logs, plans, and acceptance records must distinguish:

* detected cinematic moments;
* preferred audio-qualified moments;
* dialogue-bearing moments;
* dialogue windows examined;
* complete-shot submoments constructed;
* submoments rejected locally;
* final authored placements;
* whether deterministic rescue was invoked.

Failure wording must identify the exhausted qualification stage accurately.

8. IDENTICAL-MEDIA ANALYSIS

When source and destination hashes are identical, media inspection,
transcription, diarization, speaker attribution, visual analysis, cinematic
moments, and performances originate from one canonical analysis record.
Role-specific planning may diverge; underlying analysis may not.

9. REGRESSION REQUIREMENT

The Red Dwarf failure is a permanent regression fixture. It must demonstrate
many dialogue windows, whole-moment preference rejection, locally safe
complete-shot placements, deterministic rescue, unchanged cinematic
integrity, strict final-output acceptance, deterministic planning, and
canonical identical-media analysis reuse.

10. NON-GOALS

This authorization does not permit relaxed cinematic boundaries, arbitrary
shot subdivision, lower safety thresholds for duration, weakened final-output
acceptance, or reduced provenance.

GOVERNING PRINCIPLE

Qualify the authored placement. Do not reject an entire cinematic moment
because of unrelated audio elsewhere within it.
