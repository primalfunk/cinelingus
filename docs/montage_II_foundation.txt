CINELINGUS 0.2.x MONTAGE FOUNDATION
DESIGN AND PRODUCT AUTHORIZATION

Status

The montage direction is approved for architecture and staged implementation.

Engineering may begin immediately on:

* revised filter contracts
* the cinematic-moment schema
* shot-to-moment provenance
* conservative Phase 1 moment detection
* montage planning
* structural-role assignment
* audio-law declarations
* source-balance enforcement
* acceptance artifacts
* incremental migration of the first montage-native filters

No further creative approval is required for those areas.

1. LOCAL MODEL POLICY

Cinelingus may add local computer-vision and audio-separation models.

This authorization is subject to the following product constraints.

Offline-first

All required analysis and rendering must be capable of running locally after model installation.

No source film, extracted frame, audio stem, transcript, face representation, or derived media may be sent to a remote service by default.

Optional future cloud integrations must remain separate, explicit, and disabled by default.

Permissible uses

Local models may be used for:

* face detection and tracking
* mouth activity
* gaze direction
* pose estimation
* optical motion
* camera-motion estimation
* shot-transition classification
* stillness detection
* action-boundary evidence
* speech and voice activity
* source separation
* dialogue, music, ambience, and effects estimation

Licensing

Dependencies and model weights must permit legal redistribution or provide a clear user-managed installation path.

Engineering must record for every model:

* model name and version
* license
* source
* redistribution status
* commercial-use restrictions
* approximate download size
* expected memory requirements
* CPU support
* GPU support
* known platform limitations

A model with unclear or incompatible licensing must not become a required bundled dependency.

Installation tiers

The montage system should support three capability tiers.

Core

No heavyweight learned visual model is required.

Uses scene transitions, fades, dissolves, speech boundaries, motion, stillness, and conservative grouping.

This is the minimum supported montage capability.

Enhanced Vision

Adds face, mouth, gaze, and pose evidence.

This improves moment confidence but does not redefine the public moment contract.

Enhanced Audio

Adds local source separation and stem-aware audio rendering.

The GUI may install or enable these capabilities separately.

Cinelingus must remain functional when either enhanced tier is unavailable.

Hardware policy

CPU operation must remain possible for the Core tier.

Enhanced models may use GPU acceleration when available.

A model may not silently fail because the GPU is too small. The runtime must:

* estimate capability before execution where practical
* report the selected device
* report model fallback
* distinguish unavailable, failed, and low-confidence analysis
* preserve conservative behavior under reduced capability

No feature should require a specific GPU vendor unless explicitly marked experimental.

Footprint

Large models must be optional downloads rather than mandatory application payload.

Model files must live outside the source tree and normal run-artifact directories.

They must not be deleted by Cinelingus run cleanup.

Determinism and provenance

Every analysis artifact must record:

* requested backend
* actual backend
* model version
* device
* relevant thresholds
* confidence
* fallback path
* analysis timestamp

The same inputs, configuration, model versions, and seed should produce materially reproducible planning results.

Safety rule

Model evidence may justify accepting or rejecting a boundary.

It must never manufacture a supposedly safe boundary where confidence is insufficient.

The approved low-confidence behavior is:

1. preserve the longer complete moment;
2. choose another candidate;
3. relax target duration;
4. fall back to a simpler montage;
5. reject the montage plan.

Duration compliance is subordinate to cinematic integrity.

2. PHASED VISUAL ANALYSIS

Phase 1 is approved as the production foundation.

Phase 1 evidence may include:

* hard cuts
* dissolves
* fades
* speech starts and ends
* silence
* optical motion
* camera-motion magnitude
* local stillness
* shot duration
* adjacent-shot compatibility
* conservative moment grouping

Phase 1 must not claim to understand gestures, gaze, performance intention, or completed human action.

Its acceptance language should remain literal:

* cut-bounded
* transition-complete
* speech-complete
* low boundary motion
* stable boundary
* conservatively grouped

Phase 2 may add:

* face continuity
* mouth activity
* gaze changes
* pose changes
* gesture evidence
* action-completion evidence
* reaction-shot classification

These signals remain evidence rather than absolute truth.

The system should not expose unsupported semantic certainty such as “the gesture is complete” when it only knows that pose and motion stabilized.

3. AUDIO LAW POLICY

The long-term default remains:

Dialogue Replacement plus Continuous Ambient Bed.

However, the system must describe what it actually rendered rather than what the filter requested.

Requested Audio Law and Actual Audio Method are separate fields.

Approved actual methods include:

Stem-Separated Replacement

Dialogue is removed or suppressed using locally separated stems. Music, ambience, and usable effects are retained.

Mixed-Bed Replacement

The original mixed soundtrack is retained beneath replacement dialogue with aggressive, speech-aware attenuation.

Original Reality

The selected visual moment retains its synchronized source audio.

Generated or Constructed Bed

A separately assembled bed is used according to the filter contract.

Silence or Minimal Bed

Only the authored dialogue and deliberately retained effects are present.

Fallback hierarchy

For filters requesting Dialogue Replacement plus Continuous Ambient Bed:

1. use reliable separated ambience, music, and effects stems;
2. use a heavily ducked mixed bed when residual speech is acceptably low;
3. use Original Reality when the filter permits it;
4. use a minimal or constructed bed;
5. reject the candidate when none preserve intelligibility.

The renderer must not label mixed-source audio as a clean ambient stem.

Ghost speech is an acceptance failure when it competes with, duplicates, or contradicts the authored dialogue.

Minor indistinct vocal residue may be tolerated only when:

* it is not intelligible;
* it does not resemble a second clear line;
* it does not materially confuse speaker identity;
* the acceptance artifact records the fallback method.

Room-tone handles remain desirable, but they must be drawn from material that does not reintroduce intelligible source dialogue.

4. CALIBRATION CORPUS

A small internal calibration corpus is approved and required before declaring moment-boundary behavior production-ready.

The first corpus should contain at least:

* 20 acceptable entrances
* 20 acceptable exits
* 10 acceptable J-cuts or L-cuts
* 10 strong reaction-shot uses
* 20 visibly broken boundaries
* 5 long-take examples
* 5 dissolve examples
* 5 fade examples
* 5 speech-over-reaction examples
* 5 ambiguous or disputed examples

One example may satisfy more than one category.

Annotation unit

Each example should record:

* source identifier
* source-film identifier
* start timestamp
* end timestamp
* relevant shot IDs
* proposed visual boundary
* proposed audio boundary when different
* positive or negative label
* reason codes
* short human note
* annotator confidence
* optional preferred alternative boundary

Approved reason codes should include:

* CLEAN_CUT
* COMPLETE_SPEECH
* COMPLETE_ACTION
* LOW_BOUNDARY_MOTION
* STABLE_FRAME
* REACTION_COVERAGE
* VALID_J_CUT
* VALID_L_CUT
* TRANSITION_COMPLETE
* ESTABLISHING_VALUE
* MID_WORD
* MID_SENTENCE_ONSET
* MOUTH_INTERRUPTED
* SUBJECT_MOTION_INTERRUPTED
* CAMERA_MOTION_INTERRUPTED
* ENTERS_DURING_TRANSITION
* EXITS_DURING_TRANSITION
* VISUAL_CONTEXT_MISSING
* SOURCE_OVERDOMINANCE
* EXCESSIVE_LENGTH
* EXCESSIVE_FRAGMENTATION
* AMBIGUOUS

Annotation philosophy

The corpus is not intended to prove that there is one objectively correct edit.

It exists to establish Cinelingus’s preferred tolerance.

Disagreement is useful. Ambiguous examples should not be forced into artificial consensus.

Where two boundaries are both acceptable, both may be recorded.

Where a boundary is aesthetically strange but intentionally useful, it should be labeled acceptable only under a declared discontinuity law.

Corpus separation

The calibration material must be divided into:

* development examples
* threshold-tuning examples
* held-out acceptance examples

The held-out set must not be used to tune thresholds.

Copyright and distribution

The calibration manifest and annotations may live in the project.

Copyrighted source clips and frames should not be committed to a public repository unless distribution rights are clear.

The corpus loader should support local source paths and verify source hashes.

5. ACCEPTANCE STANDARD

Phase 1 is ready for broader use when the held-out corpus demonstrates that the system:

* does not cut inside audible words;
* does not enter or leave inside detected dissolves or fades;
* strongly prefers cut-bounded moments;
* rejects high-motion boundaries unless the filter permits discontinuity;
* preserves longer material when safe subdivision is uncertain;
* reports requested and actual audio methods honestly;
* records source, scene, shot, visual-boundary, and audio-boundary provenance;
* produces fallback plans without fabricating confidence;
* can assemble a montage from multiple distinct moments;
* can enforce required source participation;
* can complete a Quick Preview without Enhanced Vision;
* remains deterministic under fixed inputs, models, configuration, and seed.

Production readiness does not require perfect recognition of human actions.

It requires conservative behavior, traceable decisions, honest capability reporting, and visibly fewer broken edits than naive shot sampling.

6. FIRST MONTAGE-NATIVE COHORT

The approved first cohort is:

* Self Shuffle
* Flashback
* Foreshadow
* Recollection
* Dream

Recommended implementation order:

1. Self Shuffle
2. Flashback
3. Foreshadow
4. Recollection
5. Dream

Self Shuffle is the clearest architectural proving ground because it requires broad sampling from one film without immediately introducing cross-film normalization.

Flashback and Foreshadow then validate chronology and structural-role assignment.

Recollection validates repetition, partial recurrence, and memory-shaped selection.

Dream should follow only after conservative montage behavior is stable, because its permissive grammar could otherwise conceal editing defects.

7. FINAL PRODUCT PRINCIPLE

Cinelingus may use increasingly sophisticated models, but model sophistication is not the definition of quality.

The product standard remains:

Respect before Surprise.

When uncertain, preserve the cinematic moment.

When transformation conflicts with intelligibility, preserve intelligibility.

When target duration conflicts with a clean boundary, preserve the clean boundary.

When the requested audio law cannot be produced honestly, declare and use the best available fallback.

Engineering has full authority to proceed within these constraints.
